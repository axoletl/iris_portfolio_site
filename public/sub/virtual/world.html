MMORPG worlds provide context for players and their avatars interactions. More than geography in the digital instantiations of three-dimensional space, they represent the mechanics, stories and histories of the virtual environments. The creation of fantastic places is a reoccurring theme in virtual worlds given the opportunity to create a space divergent from the laws of the physical world, anything from enhanced realism to the arcane, hard science fiction to historical fantasy.

Many MMORPGs employ questing systems to involve player avatars into the stories of their worlds. Levels of involvements for these quests can vary greatly speaking from experience, anything from slaying rodents to say, travelling to a magical manifestation of ancient civilisation to overthrow a deity dedicated to the consumption of the present world in the restoration of the past.

Whatever the scenario, the stories provided by questing systems work to create a more immersive experience for the player in the virtual world, providing a grounding for social interactions between players and character lore relating to their avatars. No world is without its histories and narratives, and many MMORPGs are in understanding of this. The stories and contexts of these worlds have allowed me to feel anything from grief to joy to rage for their characters and events, providing a richness of experience to my time within them.
